## TOPIC 1: Regulatory Overview of Hong Kong Financial Industry

### 1.1 Key Regulatory Principles (Ian Hay Davison Report)

**Six Core Principles:**
1. **Systemic Stability** - Prevent market collapse
2. **Orderly Market** - Fair, free from manipulation
3. **Investor Protection** - Protect the public
4. **Practitioner-Based Regulation** - Run by industry professionals
5. **Independent Commission** - Separate from government
6. **Market Participant Representation** - On exchanges/clearing houses

### 1.2 Regulatory Philosophy

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Disclosure-Based** | Maximize information, investor decides | Companies Ordinance |
| **Merit-Based** | Screen out bad players/products | Listing Rules |
| **Risk-Based** | Focus on highest-risk areas | SFC approach |

### 1.3 Key Regulatory Bodies

| Body | Head | Role |
|------|------|------|
| **Chief Executive HKSAR** | - | Appoints SFC Chairman, CEO, Directors |
| **Financial Secretary** | - | Chairman of Exchange Fund Advisory Committee; tables SFC reports to LegCo |
| **SFC** | Chairman/CEO | Lead securities/futures regulator |
| **HKMA** | Chief Executive | Banking supervision; manages Exchange Fund |
| **MPFA** | - | Mandatory Provident Fund regulation |
| **Insurance Authority** | - | Insurance industry regulation |

### 1.4 SFC Regulatory Objectives (s.4 SFO)
1. Fairness, efficiency, competitiveness, transparency, orderliness
2. Public understanding of the industry
3. **Investor protection**
4. Minimize crime and misconduct
5. Reduce systemic risks
6. Assist Financial Secretary in financial stability

### 1.5 Professional Investor Definition
- **Corporation:** ≥HK$40 million in total assets
- **Individual (HNW):** ≥HK$8 million portfolio

## TOPIC 2: Hong Kong Law & Companies Ordinance

### 2.1 Legal System
- **Primary Legislation:** Ordinances enacted by Chief Executive with LegCo
- **Subsidiary Legislation:** Rules drafted by non-Government bodies (e.g., SFC rules)
- **Judiciary:** Completely independent of government

### 2.2 Key Companies Ordinance Concepts

| Concept | Definition | Requirement |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| **Special Resolution** | 75% majority vote | 14 days notice; filed with Registrar within 15 days |
| **Ordinary Resolution** | Simple majority (>50%) | - |
| **Redeemable Shares** | Can be bought back by company | Must be fully paid; from distributable profits/new issue |

### 2.3 Types of Shares
- **Preference Shares:** Fixed dividend, priority in winding up
- **Ordinary Shares:** Voting rights, variable dividend
- **Redeemable Shares:** Can be redeemed by company or shareholder

## TOPIC 3: Securities and Futures Ordinance (SFO)

### 3.1 SFO Objectives
- Fair, orderly, transparent market
- Flexible for new products
- Administered by independent regulator (SFC)
- On par with international standards

### 3.2 SFO Structure - 18 Parts

| Part | Subject |
|------|---------|
| Part I | Preliminary |
| Part II | SFC - Constitution, objectives, functions, powers |
| Part III | Exchange companies, clearing houses |
| Part IIIA | OTC derivative transactions |
| Part IV | Offers of investments |
| Part IVA | Open-ended fund companies (OFCs) |
| Part V | **Licensing and registration** |
| Part VI | Capital requirements, client assets |
| Part VII | Business conduct |
| Part VIII | Supervision and investigations |
| Part IX | Discipline |
| Part X | Powers of intervention |
| Part XI | Securities and Futures Appeals Tribunal |
| Part XII | **Investor compensation** |
| Part XIII | Market Misconduct Tribunal |
| Part XIV | Offences relating to dealings |
| Part XIVA | Disclosure of inside information |
| Part XV | Disclosure of interests |
| Part XVI-XVII | Miscellaneous & Repeals |

### 3.3 Key SFC Powers (Part II)

**SFC can:**
- Issue licenses/registration
- Impose conditions on licenses
- Conduct investigations
- Take disciplinary action
- Intervene in market misconduct

### 3.4 Associated Entity (AE)
**Definition:**
- Company in **controlling relationship** (>20% voting power) with intermediary
- **AND** receives/holds client assets of the intermediary in HK

**Requirements:**
- Notify SFC within **7 business days** of becoming AE
- Cannot conduct other business without SFC written approval
- Subject to Client Securities Rules

### 3.5 Regulated Activities (Type 1-12)

| Type | Activity | Capital Required |
|------|----------|------------------|
| Type 1 | Dealing in securities | Yes |
| Type 2 | Dealing in futures contracts | Yes |
| Type 3 | Leveraged foreign exchange trading | Yes |
| Type 4 | Advising on securities | No (if no client assets) |
| Type 5 | Advising on futures contracts | No (if no client assets) |
| Type 6 | Advising on corporate finance | No (if no client assets) |
| Type 7 | Providing automated trading services | Yes |
| Type 8 | Securities margin financing | Yes |
| Type 9 | Asset management | No (if no client assets) |
| Type 10 | Providing credit rating services | No |
| Type 11 | OTC derivative products/RA dealing | Yes |
| Type 12 | OTC derivative products/RA advising | No (if no client assets) |

**No capital requirement if:** Approved introducing agent, securities adviser, corporate finance adviser, asset manager, credit rating agency (and don't hold client assets)

### 3.6 Licensing Requirements

**Licensed Corporation (LC):**
- Company incorporated in HK or overseas
- Proper business structure
- Approved ROs (Responsible Officers)

**Licensed Representative (LR):**
- Individual
- Employed by LC
- Accredited to one or more regulated activities

**Responsible Officer (RO):**
- Must be approved by SFC
- Must be executive director or senior manager
- Minimum 2 ROs per LC (1 per activity)

### 3.7 Capital Requirements (Part VI / FRR)

**Formula:**
```
LIQUID CAPITAL = LIQUID ASSETS - RANKING LIABILITIES
```

**Key Points:**
- Liquid capital must be **> Required Liquid Capital (RLC)** at all times
- Liquid assets = cash and near-cash (after haircuts)
- Ranking liabilities = total liabilities - approved subordinated loans

**Notification Triggers:**
- LC < 120% of RLC → Notify SFC
- LC < 50% of last reported → Notify SFC
- Unable to comply → Notify + cease business (unless permitted)

**Paid-up Share Capital:**
- Required: HK$5m to HK$30m (depending on activities)
- **Exempt:** Introducing agents, advisers, managers without client assets

### 3.8 Client Assets Protection

**Client Securities Rules:**
- Segregate client securities from firm's assets
- Registered in segregated account or designated as trust property
- Daily reconciliation

**Client Money Rules:**
- Pay into segregated account within **1 business day**
- Cannot use client money for firm's own business
- Can only withdraw for specific purposes (settlement, margin, etc.)

### 3.9 Record Keeping

| Document | Retention Period |
|----------|------------------|
| Accounting records | 7 years |
| Client records | 7 years |
| Trading records | 7 years |
| Telephone recordings | 6 months (minimum) |

### 3.10 Investor Compensation Fund

- Covers losses from default of licensed/registered persons
- **Compensation limit:** HK$500,000 per claimant per default
- **Exclusions:** Institutional/professional investors, market losses

### 3.11 Market Misconduct (Part XIII)

**Types:**
1. **Insider dealing**
2. **False trading** (market manipulation)
3. **Price rigging**
4. **Disclosure of false/misleading info**
5. **Stock market manipulation**

**Civil vs Criminal:**
- **Market Misconduct Tribunal:** Civil proceedings, civil penalties
- **Criminal Prosecution:** Higher burden of proof, imprisonment possible

## TOPIC 4: Licensing & Subsidiary Legislation

### 4.1 Fit and Proper Criteria

**For Individuals:**
- Financial status/solvency
- Education/qualifications
- Competence
- Reputation/character/reliability
- Regulatory compliance history

**For Corporations:**
- Financial status
- Business record
- Compliance history
- Management structure

### 4.2 Approved Introducing Agent (AIA)

**Definition:** Introduces clients to other dealers but:
- Does NOT handle client assets
- Incurs NO legal liability
- Only passes offers to exchange participants

**Benefits:** Lower liquid capital, no paid-up capital requirement

### 4.3 Securities Margin Financing (SMF)

**Definition:** Lending money to:
- Acquire listed securities
- Continue holding such securities

**Exceptions (NOT SMF):**
- Underwriting/sub-underwriting
- Lending by Type 1 licensee for SMF
- Lending by CIS to finance its own investments
- Lending by AFI (bank) to clients

## TOPIC 5: Business Conduct & Client Relations

### 5.1 Code of Conduct - 12 General Principles

| GP | Principle | Key Points |
|----|-----------|------------|
| GP1 | Honesty & Fairness | Accurate info, fair charges, no misleading ads, comply with ICAC |
| GP2 | Diligence | Prompt execution, best execution, fair allocation, record calls (6 months) |
| GP3 | Capabilities | Only act within competence |
| GP4 | Information about Clients | KYC - know client's financial situation, investment objectives |
| GP5 | Information about Products | Understand products before recommending |
| GP6 | Client Agreement | Written agreement required |
| GP7 | Know Your Client | Sufficiency of information |
| GP8 | Conflict of Interest | Disclose and manage conflicts |
| GP9 | Compliance | Establish compliance procedures |
| GP10 | Responsibility of Senior Management | Ultimate responsibility |
| GP11 | Outsourcing | Remain responsible for outsourced functions |
| GP12 | Record Keeping | Maintain adequate records |

### 5.2 Fund Manager Code of Conduct (FMCC)
- Applies to Type 9 licensees
- Fiduciary duty to clients
- Fair treatment of all clients
- Conflicts of interest management
- Valuation policies

### 5.3 Suitability Requirement
**Must ensure recommendation is suitable based on:**
- Client's financial situation
- Investment experience
- Investment objectives
- Risk profile
- Time horizon

## TOPIC 6: Business Operations & Practices

### 6.1 Internal Control Guidelines - 8 Key Areas

1. Management and supervision
2. Segregation of duties
3. Personnel and training
4. Information management
5. Compliance
6. Audit
7. Operational controls
8. Risk management

### 6.2 Anti-Money Laundering (AML)

**5 Key Ordinances:**
1. **AMLO** - Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorist Financing Ordinance
2. **DTRPO** - Drug Trafficking (Recovery of Proceeds) Ordinance
3. **OSCO** - Organised and Serious Crimes Ordinance
4. **UNATMO** - UN (Anti-terrorism Measures) Ordinance
5. **WMD(CPS)O** - Weapons of Mass Destruction Ordinance

**SFC Guideline:** GAML (Guideline on AML/Counter-Terrorist Financing)

**Customer Due Diligence (CDD):**
- Identify and verify identity
- Identify beneficial owners
- Obtain info on purpose/nature of relationship
- Ongoing monitoring

**Suspicious Transaction Reports (STR):**
- Report to Joint Financial Intelligence Unit (JFIU)
- **Tipping off is an offence**

### 6.3 Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance (PDPO)

**6 Data Protection Principles:**
1. Purpose (collect for lawful purpose)
2. Accuracy and retention
3. Use (only for original purpose)
4. Security
5. Information (inform data subjects)
6. Access (allow access to own data)

**Exemptions:**
- Crime prevention/detection
- Legal proceedings
- News activities

## TOPIC 7: Participating in Hong Kong Exchanges

### 7.1 HKEX Structure

| Entity | Role |
|--------|------|
| **HKEX** | Listed holding company |
| **SEHK** | Stock Exchange of Hong Kong |
| **HKFE** | Hong Kong Futures Exchange |
| **HKCC** | HK Clearing Corporation (futures) |
| **HKSCC** | HK Securities Clearing Corporation |

### 7.2 Trading on SEHK

**Order Types:**
- **Limit Order:** At specified price or better
- **Enhanced Limit Order (ELO):** Up to 10 price queues
- **Special Limit Order (SLO):** No price limit, matches available shares

**Trading Sessions:**
| Session | Time |
|---------|------|
| Pre-opening | 9:00 - 9:30 |
| Continuous Trading (Morning) | 9:30 - 12:00 |
| Continuous Trading (Afternoon) | 13:00 - 16:00 |
| Closing Auction | 16:00 - 16:10 |

### 7.3 Traded Options
- **Call Option:** Right to BUY underlying
- **Put Option:** Right to SELL underlying
- **American Style:** Can exercise anytime
- **European Style:** Can exercise only at expiry

### 7.4 Stock Connect (Shanghai/Shenzhen)
- Northbound trading for overseas investors
- Southbound trading for mainland investors
- Trading through exchange participants

## TOPIC 8: Accessing Public Capital

### 8.1 Listing Requirements (Main Board)

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| Profit Test | 3 years ≥ HK$80m (last year ≥ HK$35m) |
| Market Cap | ≥ HK$500m at time of listing |
| Public Float | 25% (or 15-25% if market cap > HK$10bn) |
| Minimum Shareholders | 300 |

### 8.2 GEM Board
- For growth companies
- Lower requirements
- Can graduate to Main Board

### 8.3 SFC Authorized Products

- **Unit Trusts/Mutual Funds**
- **OFCs** (Open-ended Fund Companies)
- **MPF Products**
- **SFC-authorized CIS** (Collective Investment Schemes)

### 8.4 Takeovers and Mergers

**Trigger for Mandatory Offer:**
- Acquire **30% or more** of voting rights
- Hold 30-50% and acquire **>2% in 12-month period**

**Offer Price:**
- Highest price paid by offeror in previous 6 months

**Timeline:**
- Offer document within 21 days of announcement
- Offer open for at least 21 days
- Can be extended

### 8.5 Share Buy-backs
- **On-market:** Through SEHK
- **Off-market:** Outside SEHK (needs shareholder approval)

## TOPIC 9: Market Misconduct & Improper Trading

### 9.1 Market Misconduct Types

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Insider Dealing** | Trading while possessing inside information |
| **False Trading** | Creating false/misleading market appearance |
| **Price Rigging** | Manipulating prices |
| **Disclosure of False Information** | Knowingly disseminating false info |
| **Stock Market Manipulation** | Manipulating securities prices |

### 9.2 Inside Information (Part XIVA)

**Definition:**
- Specific information about corporation/listed securities
- Not generally known to public
- Would materially affect price if known

**Disclosure Obligation:**
- Listed corporations must disclose as soon as reasonably practicable
- Exemptions: Confidentiality, incomplete proposal, etc.

### 9.3 Unsolicited Calls

**Prohibited unless:**
- Client gave written consent
- Prior business relationship
- Institutional/professional investor
- **Maximum penalty:** HK$500,000 + 3 years imprisonment

### 9.4 Disclosure of Interests (Part XV)

**Major Shareholder Disclosure:**
- **5% threshold** - Disclose within 3 business days
- Subsequent changes of **1% or more** must be disclosed

**Director/CEO Disclosure:**
- Must disclose all interests in corporation's securities
- Even if <5%

### 9.5 Enforcement Actions

**SFC can:**
- Issue reprimands (public/private)
- Fines (up to HK$10m or 3x profit/loss)
- Suspension/revocation of licenses
- Prohibition orders
- Refer to criminal prosecution
