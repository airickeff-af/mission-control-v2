# Beeple (Mike Winkelmann): The Deep Dive
## From Everydays to Digital Art History

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Mike Winkelmann, professionally known as **Beeple**, is an American digital artist who transformed from a relatively unknown motion designer into one of the most valuable living artists in history—overnight. His $69.3 million NFT sale at Christie's in March 2021 didn't just make him wealthy; it fundamentally altered how the world perceives digital art, legitimized NFTs as a medium, and sparked a global conversation about the intersection of technology, creativity, and value.

This deep dive examines Beeple's complete journey: his Wisconsin upbringing, his 18+ years of daily artistic practice before NFTs existed, his explosive entry into Web3, and his current evolution as both an artist and institution-builder.

---

## PART I: PRE-WEB3 ERA (1981–2020)

### Early Life and Origins

**Birth and Family Background**

Michael Joseph Winkelmann was born on **June 20, 1981**, in **Fond du Lac, Wisconsin** (confidence: high). He grew up in **North Fond du Lac**, a small Midwestern town where his father worked as an electrical engineer and his mother worked at a senior center. This working-class, technically-inclined household would prove formative—his father taught him how to program from an early age, planting the seeds for his future digital artistry.

**Education at Purdue University**

Winkelmann attended **Purdue University** in West Lafayette, Indiana, graduating in **2003 with a degree in computer science** (confidence: high). However, his academic experience was marked by a growing disconnect between his coursework and his creative passions. He found programming "boring as shit" (his words) and increasingly devoted his time to making short films with his webcam and experimenting with digital design.

**The Origin of "Beeple"**

In **2003**, at age 22, Winkelmann adopted the moniker **"Beeple."** The name came from a **1980s stuffed toy**—a chestnut-brown, Ewok-like creature with a light-up nose produced by Carousel (confidence: high). When you covered the toy's eyes, it would emit a wild beeping sound. Winkelmann felt this interplay of vision and sound perfectly mirrored his early video work, which combined animated geometric shapes with electronic music. He now collects vintage Beeple toys from eBay and owns approximately 50 of them.

### Early Career Path

**The VJ Loops Era (2003–2009)**

After graduating, Winkelmann briefly designed corporate websites before transitioning to freelance graphics and animation work. His breakthrough came through creating **VJ loops**—short, abstract digital animations that became popular as background visuals for parties, concerts, and live events.

Between **2009 and 2019**, Beeple released hundreds of these loops under **Creative Commons licenses**, making them free for anyone to use. These clips were downloaded millions of times and used at thousands of live events worldwide. As Mark Costello, an interactive designer in Washington, D.C., recalled: "Friends and I used them a lot as the meat of the meal that we would then season with our personalized effects."

**Commercial Success Before Fame**

Contrary to the "overnight success" narrative, Beeple was already one of the highest-paid digital artists in the world before NFTs. His client roster included:

- **Music Artists**: Justin Bieber, Katy Perry, Eminem, Nicki Minaj, Zedd, One Direction, Wiz Khalifa, Janet Jackson, Imagine Dragons
- **Corporate Clients**: Apple, Nike, Samsung, Sony Pictures, Coca-Cola, SpaceX, NBC
- **Major Events**: Two Super Bowl halftime shows (including Jennifer Lopez's 2020 performance)
- **Fashion**: Louis Vuitton Spring/Summer 2019 collection (more on this below)

**The Louis Vuitton Collaboration (2019)**

In **2018–2019**, Beeple's work entered high fashion when **Nicolas Ghesquière**, creative director of Louis Vuitton, digitally printed **13 of Beeple's Everydays** onto pieces for the brand's Spring/Summer 2019 ready-to-wear collection (confidence: high). The collection debuted at Paris Fashion Week at the Louvre—a surreal experience for Winkelmann, who attended with his wife and "half expected to not see my work at all." One model after another emerged wearing his art, confirming that his digital creations had transcended the screen.

### The "Everydays" Project (2007–Present)

**Origin and Inspiration**

On **May 1, 2007**, Winkelmann began what would become his magnum opus: the **"Everydays"** project—creating and publishing one original piece of art every single day (confidence: high). The project was inspired by **Tom Judd**, an artist who drew every day for a year. Winkelmann thought this discipline would sharpen his skills and hold him accountable.

**The Rules**

The project had one simple rule: create something from start to finish every day, posted before midnight. There were no other constraints—no requirements about quality, medium, or subject matter. As Beeple explained: "In general, art is a numbers game. People don't have a lack of ideas; they have a lack of deadlines."

**Evolution of Style and Medium**

The Everydays evolved through distinct phases:

| Year | Focus | Description |
|------|-------|-------------|
| 2007–2008 | Drawing | Hand-drawn sketches on paper |
| 2009–2011 | Digital illustration | Adobe Illustrator focus |
| 2012 | Vector art | Adobe Illustrator year |
| 2015–present | 3D/Cinema 4D | Transition to 3D rendering with Octane |
| 2023–present | Fundamentals | Color, composition, value study |

**The Discipline**

Beeple has maintained this practice through:
- His wedding day
- The births of his children
- Food poisoning
- Family vacations
- The $69 million Christie's auction week

As of early 2026, he has completed **over 6,000 consecutive days** of the project.

**Aesthetic Development**

Beeple's mature style—developed around **2017** when he began using stock 3D models from sites like TurboSquid—combines:
- **Dystopian futures** and post-apocalyptic themes
- **Political and social satire** using pop culture figures
- **Grotesque, surreal imagery** (lactating cyborgs, zombie celebrities)
- **Current events commentary** (often created same-day)

His stated goal: "Make something that is so weird and so out-there that it makes you just think of questions instead of answers."

### Pre-NFT Financial Situation

Before NFTs, Beeple was already financially successful but not wealthy by art-world standards. He:
- Lived in a **plain four-bedroom house** outside Charleston, South Carolina
- Worked from a home studio with "wall-to-wall beige carpeting, Walmart bookshelves, and two sixty-five-inch televisions"
- Made "good money" from commercial work but nothing approaching millions
- Released his VJ loops for free under Creative Commons
- Had approximately **2 million Instagram followers** by 2020

## PART II: THE MFIGGE CONNECTION

### Michael Figge and WENEW

**Who is Michael Figge?**

**Michael Figge** (often referred to as "mfigge" in crypto circles) is a creative director and entrepreneur who became Beeple's business partner in the Web3 space (confidence: high). Figge's background includes:

- Co-founder and Creative Director of **Possible**, an experiential design studio
- Work on immersive visual experiences for the **Super Bowl halftime show**, **Coachella**, **Louis Vuitton**, and **League of Legends Worlds** (for which he won an **Emmy in 2021**)
- Deep experience in concert visuals and brand experiences

**WENEW: The Company**

In **2021**, Beeple and Michael Figge co-founded **WENEW**, an NFT powerhouse focused on "web3-centric partnerships" with major brands (confidence: high). The company specialized in:

- High-profile brand collaborations (Louis Vuitton, Playboy, Wimbledon, Gucci, Puma)
- Narrative-driven NFT collections
- Building bridges between traditional brands and Web3

**10KTF: The Flagship Collection**

WENEW's most notable creation was **10KTF**, an interoperable digital storefront where holders of top NFT collections (Bored Ape Yacht Club, Cool Cats, Moonbirds, etc.) could mint unique digital wearables featuring their avatars. The collection was built around a fictional character named **Wagmi-san** and gained popularity through serialized, narrative-driven content.

**Acquisition by Yuga Labs (November 2022)**

In **November 2022**, **Yuga Labs**—the company behind Bored Ape Yacht Club—acquired WENEW and 10KTF (confidence: high). Key details:
- **Michael Figge joined Yuga Labs as Chief Content Officer**
- **Beeple joined Yuga Labs as an advisor**
- Nearly 20 WENEW employees moved to Yuga
- The acquisition brought Yuga's full-time staff to over 100 people

Greg Solano, co-founder of Yuga Labs, stated: "Figge, Beeple, and the WENEW team have found a way to create a captivating serialized story for web3, while also managing to tap into the passion people have for their digital avatars and customization."

## PART III: WEB3/FAME ERA (2020–Present)

### Discovery of NFTs

**October 2020: The Introduction**

Beeple was introduced to NFTs in **October 2020** by **Pak**, an anonymous digital artist who was already successfully selling NFTs (confidence: high). Pak provided Beeple's "primer" on the technology, patiently answering what Beeple later called "all my noob questions."

Initially skeptical—dismissing NFTs as "some weird crypto thing"—Beeple became intrigued when he recognized names of artists he respected who were already selling work in the space.

**The First Drops: Testing the Waters**

**October 30, 2020**: Beeple launched his first NFT "drop" on **Nifty Gateway**, testing his marketability with three works:

1. **"Politics Is Bullshit"** — A diarrheic bull draped in an American flag, surrounded by dollar bills (edition of 100, sold for $1 each; later resold for up to $600,000)
2. **"Crossroad"** — A dynamic NFT that would change based on the 2020 U.S. presidential election outcome
3. **"Crypto Is Bullshit"** — A commentary on cryptocurrency skepticism

**"Crossroad" and the Election**

"Crossroad" was designed to display one of two animations depending on whether Trump or Biden won the 2020 election. It originally sold for **$66,666.66** and was resold in **February 2021 for $6.7 million**—a 100x return in months (confidence: high).

**December 2020: The MF Collection**

Beeple's second drop included **"The Complete MF Collection,"** a selection of Everydays images sold with physical "physicals"—digital frames containing the artwork and, as proof of authenticity, a sample of Beeple's actual hair. The winning bid: **$777,777** (confidence: high).

### The Christie's Sale: "Everydays: The First 5000 Days"

**The Announcement (February 2021)**

On **February 25, 2021**, Christie's announced it would auction **"Everydays: The First 5000 Days"**—a collage of the first 5,000 images from Beeple's daily project (confidence: high). This marked:
- The first purely digital NFT sold by a legacy auction house
- The first time Christie's accepted cryptocurrency (Ether) as payment
- Bidding started at just **$100**

**The Auction (March 11, 2021)**

The sale concluded on **March 11, 2021**, with a final price of **$69,346,250** (confidence: high). The bidding exploded in the final minutes, jumping in $10–15 million increments.

**Historic Significance**:
- Third-highest price for a living artist at auction (behind Jeff Koons and David Hockney)
- Most expensive NFT ever sold at the time
- Highest price for any lot in an online-only auction
- 91% of bidders were new to Christie's
- 58% of bidders were Millennials

**The Buyer: Vignesh Sundaresan (MetaKovan)**

The winning bidder was **Vignesh Sundaresan**, a Singapore-based blockchain entrepreneur also known as **"MetaKovan"** (confidence: high). He paid using **42,329 Ether**. Sundaresan stated: "This is going to be a billion-dollar piece someday. This has the potential to be the work of art of this generation."

**Beeple's Reaction**

Winkelmann was stunned. The day after the sale, he told The New Yorker: "I went outside and I was just fucking crying, man." He immediately converted the Ether to U.S. dollars (approximately $53 million after fees), explaining: "I'm not remotely a crypto-purist. I was making digital art long before any of this shit, and if all this fucking NFT stuff went away tomorrow I would still be making digital art."

### Post-Sale Major Works

**"Human One" (November 2021)**

On **November 9, 2021**, Beeple sold **"Human One"** at Christie's for **$28,958,000** (confidence: high). This work represented a significant evolution:
- A **7-foot-high physical sculpture** with four LED screens
- A **generative, dynamically changing** digital component
- Described as "the first portrait of a human born in the metaverse"
- Beeple can update the imagery remotely via the internet
- Purchased by collector **Ryan Zurrer**

"Human One" has been exhibited globally:
- Castello di Rivoli Museum of Contemporary Art (Turin, 2022)
- M+ Museum (Hong Kong, 2022–2023)
- Crystal Bridges Museum of American Art (Arkansas, 2023–2024)
- Mori Art Museum (Tokyo, 2025)

**"The Tree of Knowledge" (2024)**

Unveiled at **Italian Tech Week** in September 2024, this generative sculpture features four video screens forming a rectangular pillar displaying an endless video of a tree intertwined with industrial elements. The work is influenced by real-time data (news channels, stock tickers, crypto prices) and allows viewers to adjust the display from "Signal" (order) to "Noise" (chaos).

The piece was sold to **Lingotto Investment Management**, with proceeds supporting non-profit initiatives for food justice and environmental protection.

**"Diffuse Control" (2025)**

A collaborative, kinetic sculpture that examines "distributed authorship and mass collaboration through an evolving artificial intelligence system." The work operates across three layers of control shared by the artist, curators, and viewers. Exhibited at:
- Beeple Studios
- Los Angeles County Museum of Art (LACMA) — October 2025 to January 2026
- The Shed (New York City) — June to September 2025

**"Transient Bloom" (2025)**

Revealed at the **Toledo Museum of Art** on **September 13, 2025**, this large-scale generative digital environment explores "impermanence, collective memory, and distributed authorship through artificial intelligence." Drawing inspiration from Claude Monet's studies of light, the work presents a continuously evolving garden where organic forms merge with digital culture imagery.

**"Regular Animals" (Art Basel Miami 2025)**

At **Art Basel Miami Beach** (December 3–7, 2025), Beeple presented an installation featuring **animatronic robot dogs** with hyper-realistic heads resembling tech figures (Elon Musk, Jeff Bezos, Mark Zuckerberg) and artists (Pablo Picasso, Andy Warhol, Beeple himself). The robots photographed their surroundings and produced printed images from their rear ends, which were minted as NFTs. Editions were priced around **$100,000** each.

### Beeple Studios (2021–Present)

**The Space**

In **fall 2021**, Beeple established **Beeple Studios** in **Charleston, South Carolina**—a **50,000-square-foot facility** described as "a weird mix of Warhol's studio and Bell Labs" (confidence: high). The space includes:
- Offices and lab spaces
- A white wall gallery
- An immersive experiential space for showcasing digital art

**Grand Opening (March 11, 2023)**

The studio held its grand opening on the **two-year anniversary** of the Christie's sale, hosting "the biggest names in the crypto, NFT, and traditional art worlds." The event featured massive projections, community art showcases, and live performances.

**Mission**

Beeple Studios serves as:
- A venue for digital art events and exhibitions
- An incubator for new ideas in digital art presentation
- A community hub for artists and technologists
- A prototype for what museums could become in the digital age

## PART V: HOW BEEPLE CHANGED DIGITAL ART

### Legitimization of Digital Art

Before Beeple, digital art existed in a strange limbo—it was ubiquitous (in movies, apps, games, advertising) yet rarely considered "fine art." Beeple's Christie's sale forced a reckoning:

- **Museum acquisitions**: LACMA, SFMOMA, Centre Pompidou, Buffalo AKG, and others began acquiring NFTs
- **Auction house acceptance**: Christie's, Sotheby's, and Phillips now regularly hold digital art auctions
- **Critical discourse**: Art critics and historians were forced to engage with digital art as a legitimate medium

### Democratization of Art Discovery

Beeple's rise challenged the traditional gallery system:

- He amassed **millions of followers** without any institutional validation
- His success came through social media, not gallery representation
- He proved that artists could build audiences directly, bypassing gatekeepers
- As he stated: "I used social media to convince millions of people of the value of my work—millions of people who don't have a bunch of power. They're just an average person who's like, 'Hey, this is interesting.'"

### New Models of Art Ownership

Beeple pioneered:
- **Dynamic NFTs** that change over time ("Crossroad," "Human One")
- **Physical-digital hybrids** that exist in both realms
- **Generative, evolving sculptures** that the artist can update remotely
- **Community-curated exhibitions** at Beeple Studios

### The "Everydays" as Artistic Methodology

Beeple's daily practice has inspired countless artists:
- Demonstrates the power of consistency over talent alone
- Shows how quantity leads to quality through iteration
- Proves that sharing work-in-progress builds audience
- Establishes a model for "public art" through social media

### Institutional Innovation

Through Beeple Studios, he's prototyping:
- Museums that can rapidly curate hundreds of artists
- Experiential spaces that change constantly
- Community-driven exhibition models
- Integration of digital and physical art experiences

## CONCLUSION: THE BEEPLE LEGACY

Mike Winkelmann's journey from a Wisconsin computer science graduate to one of the most valuable living artists is a story of persistence, timing, and technological disruption. His 18 years of daily artistic practice built the foundation; NFTs provided the mechanism for monetization; but it was his authentic, weird, and unapologetically digital vision that captured the world's attention.

Beeple didn't just sell a JPEG for $69 million—he:
- Legitimized digital art as a medium worthy of museum acquisition
- Demonstrated that artists could build careers outside traditional gatekeepers
- Pioneered new forms of physical-digital hybrid art
- Created a model for daily creative practice that has inspired thousands
- Built an institution (Beeple Studios) that prototypes the future of art presentation

Whether "Everydays: The First 5000 Days" ever regains its $69 million valuation is irrelevant to Beeple's place in art history. He has already changed how we think about digital creativity, ownership, and value. As he himself said: "I believe we are witnessing the beginning of the next chapter in art history, digital art."

Four years after that statement, the chapter is still being written—and Beeple remains one of its most important authors.
