TOPIC 1 Regulatory Overview of HK Financial Industry

🎯 Six Core Principles (Ian Hay Davison Report)

  1. Systemic Stability - Prevent market collapse
  2. Orderly Market - Fair, free from manipulation
  3. Investor Protection - Protect the public
  4. Practitioner-Based Regulation - Run by industry professionals
  5. Independent Commission - Separate from government
  6. Market Participant Representation - On exchanges/clearing houses

Regulatory Philosophy

TypeDescriptionExample
Disclosure-BasedMaximize information, investor decidesCompanies Ordinance
Merit-BasedScreen out bad players/productsListing Rules
Risk-BasedFocus on highest-risk areasSFC approach

SFC Regulatory Objectives (s.4 SFO)

  1. Fairness, efficiency, competitiveness, transparency, orderliness
  2. Public understanding of the industry
  3. Investor protection
  4. Minimize crime and misconduct
  5. Reduce systemic risks
  6. Assist Financial Secretary in financial stability

Professional Investor Definition

  • Corporation: ≥HK$40 million in total assets
  • Individual (HNW): ≥HK$8 million portfolio

TOPIC 2 HK Law & Companies Ordinance

Legal System

  • Primary Legislation: Ordinances enacted by Chief Executive with LegCo
  • Subsidiary Legislation: Rules drafted by non-Government bodies (e.g., SFC rules)
  • Judiciary: Completely independent of government

Key Companies Ordinance Concepts

ConceptDefinitionRequirement
Special Resolution75% majority vote14 days notice; filed within 15 days
Ordinary ResolutionSimple majority (>50%)-
Redeemable SharesCan be bought back by companyMust be fully paid; from distributable profits/new issue

Types of Shares

  • Preference Shares: Fixed dividend, priority in winding up
  • Ordinary Shares: Voting rights, variable dividend
  • Redeemable Shares: Can be redeemed by company or shareholder

Associated Entity (AE)

Definition:
  • Company in controlling relationship (>20% voting power) with intermediary
  • AND receives/holds client assets of the intermediary in HK
Requirements:
  • Notify SFC within 7 business days of becoming AE
  • Cannot conduct other business without SFC written approval

Capital Requirements Formula

LIQUID CAPITAL = LIQUID ASSETS − RANKING LIABILITIES
  • Liquid capital must be > Required Liquid Capital (RLC) at all times
  • Liquid assets = cash and near-cash (after haircuts)
  • Ranking liabilities = total liabilities − approved subordinated loans

Notification Triggers

TriggerAction
LC < 120% of RLCNotify SFC
LC < 50% of last reportedNotify SFC
Unable to complyNotify + cease business (unless permitted)

Client Assets Protection

Client Securities: Segregate from firm's assets, registered in segregated account, daily reconciliation

Client Money: Pay into segregated account within 1 business day, cannot use for firm's business

Investor Compensation Fund

  • Covers losses from default of licensed/registered persons
  • Compensation limit: HK$500,000 per claimant per default
  • Exclusions: Institutional/professional investors, market losses

TOPIC 4 Licensing & Subsidiary Legislation

Fit and Proper Criteria

For Individuals:

  • Financial status/solvency
  • Education/qualifications
  • Competence
  • Reputation/character/reliability
  • Regulatory compliance history

Approved Introducing Agent (AIA)

Definition: Introduces clients to other dealers but:
  • Does NOT handle client assets
  • Incurs NO legal liability
  • Only passes offers to exchange participants
Benefits: Lower liquid capital, no paid-up capital requirement

Securities Margin Financing (SMF)

Definition: Lending money to acquire/continue holding listed securities

Exceptions (NOT SMF):

  • Underwriting/sub-underwriting
  • Lending by Type 1 licensee for SMF
  • Lending by CIS to finance its own investments
  • Lending by AFI (bank) to clients

Suitability Requirement

Must ensure recommendation is suitable based on:
  • Client's financial situation
  • Investment experience
  • Investment objectives
  • Risk profile
  • Time horizon

Anti-Money Laundering (AML)

5 Key Ordinances:
  1. AMLO - Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorist Financing Ordinance
  2. DTRPO - Drug Trafficking (Recovery of Proceeds) Ordinance
  3. OSCO - Organised and Serious Crimes Ordinance
  4. UNATMO - UN (Anti-terrorism Measures) Ordinance
  5. WMD(CPS)O - Weapons of Mass Destruction Ordinance
SFC Guideline: GAML (Guideline on AML/Counter-Terrorist Financing)

Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

  • Identify and verify identity
  • Identify beneficial owners
  • Obtain info on purpose/nature of relationship
  • Ongoing monitoring
Suspicious Transaction Reports (STR): Report to Joint Financial Intelligence Unit (JFIU).
TIPPING OFF IS AN OFFENCE!

Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance (PDPO)

6 Data Protection Principles:

  1. Purpose (collect for lawful purpose)
  2. Accuracy and retention
  3. Use (only for original purpose)
  4. Security
  5. Information (inform data subjects)
  6. Access (allow access to own data)

TOPIC 8 Accessing Public Capital

Listing Requirements (Main Board)

RequirementStandard
Profit Test3 years ≥ HK$80m (last year ≥ HK$35m)
Market Cap≥ HK$500m at time of listing
Public Float25% (or 15-25% if market cap > HK$10bn)
Minimum Shareholders300

Takeovers and Mergers

Trigger for Mandatory Offer:
  • Acquire 30% or more of voting rights
  • Hold 30-50% and acquire >2% in 12-month period
Offer Price: Highest price paid by offeror in previous 6 months

Share Buy-backs

  • On-market: Through SEHK
  • Off-market: Outside SEHK (needs shareholder approval)

Inside Information (Part XIVA)

Definition:
  • Specific information about corporation/listed securities
  • Not generally known to public
  • Would materially affect price if known
Disclosure Obligation: Listed corporations must disclose as soon as reasonably practicable

Unsolicited Calls

Prohibited unless:
  • Client gave written consent
  • Prior business relationship
  • Institutional/professional investor
Maximum penalty: HK$500,000 + 3 years imprisonment
